Who We Are

Fully funded by Conservation Leadership Programme (CLP) 2015 Future Conservation Award and in collaboration with Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fisheries (KKP) Republic of Indonesia, Bogor Agricultural University (IPB), and WWF-Indonesia, we organize trainings for local people to increase their expertise in monitoring whale shark population in their own area.

We work closely with local stakeholders, mainly from one of the local offices of KKP, the Center for Regional office of Coastal and Marine Resource (BPSPL), of which main obligation is to conduct survey on protected marine species, and local universities to ensure knowledge transfer and continuation of the program at local level. In Aceh, we work with BPSPL Padang and University of Syiah Kuala. In Probolinggo, we will work with BPSPL Denpasar and Brawijaya University. In Talisayan, we work together with Balikpapan and Mulawarwan University. Lastly, in Parigi, we work with BPSPL Makassar, Sam Ratulangi University and local dive resort (Parigi Mountong Resort).

Through Facebook fanpage, we also raise awareness on the importance of protecting whale sharks and respecting the marine environment.

Why We Do

Whale shark, the largest fish in the world, has been listed as Vulnerable by the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN). In Indonesia, the growing public interest of whale shark was triggered by the discovery of whale shark affiliation with lift-net fisheries in Cenderawasih Bay, Papua, in 2006. The preliminary study that was conducted there had become one foundation for KKP to declare whale shark as a fully protected fish in Indonesia by May 2013. However, there are still very few studies about whale shark in Indonesia despite its current status and large size.

What We Do

Some of our team members have worked with whale sharks in Cenderawasih Bay, West Papua, during 2013-2014. Based on the successful experience, we plan to expand our study by conducting and facilitate a whale shark study and conservation during 2015-2016 in four important sites where whale sharks have been sighted regularly;Weh Island (Aceh), Probolinggo (East Java), Talisayan (East Kalimantan) and Parigi (Central Sulawesi). Together with local stakeholders, we will conduct preliminary monitoring (individual identification and water quality measurement), facilitate monitoring training, and gather information as much as possible about whale shark sightings and stranding events in Indonesian waters. All information will be published here eventually as the website was also planned to be an online database which is accessible for everyone who is interested. These efforts hopefully will be the embryo for the birth of the Whale Shark Research Centre in Indonesia.
