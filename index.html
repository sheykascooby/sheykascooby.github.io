Whale Shark Indonesia Project

"Eat, sleep, and whale shark!"

Read More

Whale Shark Indonesia Project (WSIP) is a team of young researchers, marine conservation professionals, and communication experts who work together to protect marine biodiversity with focus on both whale shark and its habitat. Most of our scientific research are emphasized on whale shark population genetics and distribution.

Whale Sharks in Indonesia

In Indonesia, the growing public interest of whale shark was triggered by the discovery of whale shark affiliation with lift-net fisheries in Cenderawasih Bay, Papua, in 2006.

Learn more

Our Research

Together with local stakeholders, we will conduct preliminary monitoring (individual identification and water quality measurement), facilitate monitoring training, and gather information as much as possible about whale shark sightings and stranding events in Indonesian waters.

How You Can Help

Our volunteers bring a whole range of different skills and interests to Whale Shark Indonesia Project and you do not have to be a wildlife or whale shark specialist to help.

Every year we will gather and write a report about these opportunistic sightings and strandings of whale shark to the Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fisheries Republic of Indonesia.
