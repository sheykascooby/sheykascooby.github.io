Who

Whale Shark Indonesia Project (WSIP) is a team of young researchers, marine conservation professionals, and communication experts who work together to protect marine biodiversity with focus on both whale shark and its habitat. Most of our scientific research are emphasized on whale shark population genetics and distribution.

Funded by Conservation Leadership Programme (CLP) 2015 Future Conservation Award and in collaboration with Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fisheries (MMAF) Republic of Indonesia, Bogor Agricultural University (IPB), and WWF-Indonesia, we organize trainings for local people to increase their expertise in monitoring whale shark population in their own area. We will work closely with local stakeholders, mainly from Center for Regional office of Coastal and Marine Resource (CRCMR), whose main obligation is to conduct survey on protected marine species, and local universities to ensure knowledge transfer and continuation of the program at local level. In Aceh, we will work with Padang CRCMR and University of Syiah Kuala. In Probolinggo, we will work with Denpasar CRCMR and Brawijaya University. In Talisayan, we will work together with Balikpapan CRCMR and Mulawarwan University. Lastly, in Parigi, we will work with Makassar CRCMR, Sam Ratulangi University and local dive resort (Parigi Mountong Resort).

Through a variety of media, we also raise awareness on the importance of protecting whale sharks and respecting the marine environment.
